Day 3: Art Pop & Fable Night

February 25, 2026 CJ Mape 5 min read adventures, gaming

Another day at the Art Pop Market with Elaine, then more Fable when I got home. Today was a good one.

Back to the Market

Elaine and I went back to the Legazpi Art Pop Market today. It's becoming our little thing, I guess. We took our time exploring all the booths – didn't rush like last time. The weather was nice, and there were more vendors than before.

There was live music too – a local band playing acoustic covers. Good vibes all around. The market just hits different on a nice afternoon.

Vintage Game Art

I found that booth again with the vintage video game art prints. The one I was eyeing last time was still there. This time I actually bought it – a pixel art print of an old RPG scene. Gonna put it up in my room.

The artist was really friendly and talked to us about how she started making prints. Always cool to hear people's stories. Elaine got herself a keychain made from recycled materials – a little fish that reminds her of home. Supporting local artists feels good.

Photobooth Fun

We passed by the photobooth again and couldn't resist taking another set of photos. This time we were sillier – funny faces and all. The photobooth strips are starting to pile up on my bulletin board. In a good way, I think. It's nice having physical memories like that.

Street Food

Grabbed some fish balls and kwek-kwek before heading home. Hit the spot. Hopefully no upset stomach this time! We sat on a bench and ate while watching people pass by. Simple pleasures.

Fable: The Lost Chapters

When I got home, I jumped back into Fable. Made some progress in the main story and did a few more side quests. I'm trying to be a good character in this playthrough – we'll see how long that lasts. The game has so much charm.

There's just something about this game. The humor, the choices, the world. It's comfort gaming for sure. I'm already thinking about what build I want to try next.

Good day with friends, good night with games. No complaints.

Days like this remind me why I love documenting this journey. The small moments add up.

— CJ
